### ğŸ”‘ Key Concepts

* **Sampling error**: The difference between an estimate from a sample and the true population parameter caused by chance variation from sampling.
* **Sampling distribution**: A theoretical distribution of estimates obtained from all possible samples of the same size from the population.
* **Standard error (SE)**: The standard deviation of the sampling distribution; it describes how much estimates typically vary due to sampling error.
* **Law of Large Numbers**: As sample size increases, estimates converge on the true population parameter.
* **File drawer problem**: The tendency for extreme results from small samples to be published while boring or inconclusive results remain unpublished, biasing the scientific literature.
