### ğŸ”‘ Key Concepts & Definitions

* **Sampling**: Selecting a subset of individuals from a population to estimate something about the whole.
* **Population parameter**: A true (but often unknowable) number that describes the population, e.g., the actual average petal area.
* **Sample estimate**: A number calculated from a sample to approximate a population parameter.
* **Sampling error**: The unavoidable difference between a sample estimate and the population parameter, due to random chance.
* **Random sampling**: Every individual has an equal and independent chance of being selected.
* **Stratified random sampling**: Break the population into subgroups (e.g., shady vs sunny slopes), then sample randomly within each.
* **Haphazard sampling**: Sampling based on convenience or intuitionâ€”less ideal, but usable if acknowledged.
